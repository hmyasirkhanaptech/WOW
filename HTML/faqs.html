Wonders of the World - FAQ

1. What are the seven wonders of the Ancient world?
The seven wonders of the Ancient world
  • Great Pyramid of Giza
  • Hanging Gardens of Babylon
  • Statue of Zeus at Olympia
  • Temple of Artemis at Ephesus
  • Mausoleum at Halicarnassus
  • Colossus of Rhodes
  • Lighthouse of Alexandria
2. What are the new seven wonders of the world?
The New Seven Wonders of the World, chosen in 2007, are:
  • Great Pyramid of Giza
  • Hanging Gardens of Babylon
  • Statue of Zeus at Olympia
  • Temple of Artemis at Ephesus
  • Mausoleum at Halicarnassus
  • Colossus of Rhodes
  • Lighthouse of Alexandria
3.Are there any natural wonders of world?
Yes, there are seven natural wonders of the world, which include:
  • Mount Everest
  • Great Barrier Reef
  • Victoria Falls
  • Grand Canyon
  • Aurora Borealis (Northern Lights)
  • Paricutin Volcano
  • Harbor of Rio de Janeiro
4.How long is the Great Wall of China?
The Great Wall spans approximately 21,196 kilometers (13,171 miles). It was built over several dynasties to protect against invasions.
5. What is Petra, and why is it famous?
Petra, located in Jordan, is an ancient city carved into rose-red cliffs. It was a significant trading hub and is known for structures like Al-Khazneh (the Treasury).
6.How was Christ the Redeemer built?
The Christ the Redeemer statue in Rio de Janeiro, Brazil, was constructed between 1922 and 1931. It stands 30 meters (98 feet) tall and was built with reinforced concrete and soapstone.
7. What is Machu Picchu?
Machu Picchu is a 15th-century Incan citadel in Peru, located high in the Andes Mountains. It is famous for its remarkable stone construction and stunning mountainous setting.

9. What is the Colosseum used for today?
The Roman Colosseum in Italy is a historic amphitheater primarily used for tours, exhibitions, and cultural events. It was originally built for gladiator games and public spectacles.
10. Why is the Taj Mahal called a "monument of love"?
The Taj Mahal in India was built by Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan as a mausoleum for his wife, Mumtaz Mahal. Its intricate white marble architecture symbolizes eternal love.
